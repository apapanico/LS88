3. Offensive Eras in Baseball¶

Baseball is known to have offensive "eras". The Dead-Ball era was an era of low offensive performance from about 1900-1920. The Live-Ball era started around 1920 and it is believed this was due to a change in the manufacturing of the ball (See ringer.com). The 1960s featured a dramatic tilt towards pitchers with the mound being lowered in 1969. More recently we had the 1990s and early 2000s Steroid era. Most recent is the 2010s decline and then resurgence (again, see ringer.com and 538.com).

To what extent to each of the classical statistics indicate the variability in performance across these eras?

4. Stabilized Play¶

Looking again at the historical view of the classical stats, it appears as though the game has stabilized considerably since pre-1900 (the relative swings across eras are not nearly as large in magnitude).

What might be happening in the early days of baseball (the World Series and the modern leagues weren't taking shape until after 1900)? Why is a stabilizing effect like this not surprising?
